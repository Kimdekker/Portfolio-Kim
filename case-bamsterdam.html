B. Amsterdam

Rent your event space at B Amsterdam events

Challenge

My task was to provide the wireframe (given by the clients UX team) for B. Amsterdam's new events website, a design finish with the existing branding guidelines. The website needed to enable visitors to easily contact B. Amsterdam to rent event spaces and obtain detailed information about specific spaces available.

after, the subsequent challenge was to build the website with help of a CMS. The CMS needed to be user-friendly, allowing employees of B. Amsterdam to effortlessly add and modify content while adhering strictly to the brand guidelines outlined in the brand book, which means I had to build a template system.

My part

Taking on B. Amsterdam as my own first client during my internship was a significant responsibility. I was directly in contact with them, responsible for addressing any issues that arose throughout the project. Working with such a prestigious client was both challenging and immensely rewarding, providing invaluable experience and professional growth opportunities.

Giving the wireframe color

During the user interface design phase, I had a good look on the existing brandstyling, and styled the given wireframe so the B. styling is recognizable.

In the styling, that consists of black and white blocky features, I had to do something that makes the special events website stand out. Users have to see that they are scrolling through the B. events page. I solved this by giving color to the different categories within B. As they really liked that idea, I went out and gave the events page an orange accent.

My spin on the brandbook

Ensuring a consistent UI was paramount to convey the authenticity of the B. Amsterdam website. However, I faced the challenge of making the events page stand out within the brand guidelines.

Through some iteration a talk with the client, I decided to infuse the events page with its own distinct style. This involved adopting a slightly more creative and relaxed layout, reflecting the dynamic nature of events compared to traditional business meetings or office settings.

Using a CMS to build

B. Amsterdam sought a website that could be effortlessly updated and maintained, leading us to opt for a CMS solution. After deliberation, we decided on a custom WordPress setup.

Building the CMS

The building plan entailed my responsibility for setting up the system, constructing the pages based on my designs, and developing a custom post type for the available rental spaces. It was crucial to ensure that the client could easily create additional pages without disrupting the custom template Iv'e made for them. This way the client can go it's own way in keeping the website up to date after my work is done.

Custom CSS

The design and B.'s design patterns made me use a lot of custom CSS and had to work together with the custom template structured elements, ensuring an environment that is as structured as possible.

Finishing up

Upon completing the website build, I provided comprehensive instructions on website maintenance. This included guidance on updating content, adding new rental spaces, creating new pages, and utilizing building blocks effectively. By empowering the client with these skills, I ensured they could confidently manage and expand the website in the future.

The result

The outcome of my efforts is a user-friendly website that mirrors the B. Amsterdam brand, while also being easily maintainable and adaptable. The B. team expressed their satisfaction with the final product, underscoring its effectiveness and efficiency.

Since the day I finished up, the B. team has been flawlessly using my work untill this day.
